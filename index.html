Voice of the customer

What we're hearing from customer interviews

Six use case interviews completed across recruiters, HR ops, TA partners, and HR managers. No demos shown — pure pain validation.

Key highlights across all interviews

■

Manual review is the real time killer. Screening filters exist in every ATS, but recruiters still spend most of their time manually reading resumes to understand who the candidate is. They want AI to summarize the story — background, reasons for leaving, what they’re looking for — not just match keywords.

■

AI must assist, never decide. Every single participant (6/6) was clear: AI should present pros and cons, not make recommendations. Even labeling a candidate “Strong Hire” was flagged as biasing the rest of the cycle. Compliance screening must stay human-driven.

■

Scheduling pain is really a hiring manager behavior problem. The friction isn’t always the calendar tool — it’s hiring managers who don’t keep calendars updated and cancel after scheduling is done. Recruiters build manual workarounds to protect candidate experience at the cost of their own time.

■

Every ATS is missing something different. No one is happy with their current system. Jobvite has zero AI. Greenhouse doesn’t connect to contracts. UKG was picked for payroll, not recruiting. Ashby can’t save template drafts. The opportunity is a unified platform that closes these fragmented gaps.

■

AI-generated fraud is emerging fast. Two participants flagged identity fraud in remote/global hiring — candidates who aren’t who they say they are. Current workarounds are entirely manual: one-way video interviews, LinkedIn cross-referencing, IP address checking.

■

Carlin (internal recruiter) validates the same pain. Internal recruiting echoes these findings — screening volume overwhelms, scheduling coordination is a tax, and the desire is for AI to surface what matters so recruiters can focus on the human judgment that actually moves hires forward.

Finding 1: Manual review is the real time sink

5 of 6 participants

Screening filters are obvious and exist in current tools. What eats time is manual review to understand who the candidate is — not whether they pass filters.

Recruiters want to see: a simple story about the candidate, reasons for leaving, what they’re looking for, and a narrative for the hiring manager.

“If AI was able to even summarize their CV, their background… reasons for leaving… kind of like giving a story to the recruiter, I think that would be really good.”

Finding 2: AI must assist, not decide

6 of 6 participants — unanimous

AI should present pros and cons, not recommendations. First-level screening with AI is fine, but always with a human in the loop.

Even using jargon like “Strong Hire” was flagged as potentially biasing. Compliance screening is too sensitive to delegate to AI.

“We can’t have the agent make a decision, because of biases that aren’t neutralized there. We want it to summarize, show us pros and cons… the manager makes the decision.”

Finding 3: Scheduling pain = hiring manager behavior

5 of 6 participants

All five who discussed scheduling in depth described significant time spent. The real friction: hiring managers who don’t keep calendars updated and cancel after scheduling is done.

Some recruiters do pre-confirmation with the interview team just to make sure they’ll actually show up.

“Sometimes hiring managers may not be great at updating their calendars… you finally get time, you put it on your calendars, and then two minutes later… oh, I had a doctor’s appointment.”

Finding 4: Everyone wants less admin — “craft” differs by role

All participants

HR Ops

Daily candidate experience reviews, reading feedback, working with the team on optimizing.

Recruiters

More time in actual screening conversations and staying on top of the pipeline proactively — not playing catch-up.

HR Manager

Making onboarding simpler and time for managerial work.

Finding 5: Every ATS is missing something different

All participants

Kierra (Jobvite): Zero AI — reviews every applicant by hand.

Harveen (Greenhouse): Doesn’t connect to contract system. Offer data re-entered manually every time.

Gal: Offer-to-close fully manual — ATS, Slack for approvals, and contract templates are all disconnected.

Katie (UKG): Stuck with a system picked for payroll, not recruiting. Offer editing, sourcing, and scheduling are all harder than her previous system.

Cassius (Ashby): Has to leave Ashby to check room availability in Google Calendar. Can’t save template drafts.

Deborah: One hire who can’t complete electronic paperwork on their phone blocks the entire onboarding pipeline.

Finding 6: AI-driven identity fraud

2 of 6 participants — emerging problem

Identity fraud is a growing challenge in remote/global hiring that no tool is currently solving. Recruiters are building manual verification methods from scratch.

Current workarounds: one-way video interviews → LinkedIn profile cross-referencing (profile age, connections, education match) → employment verification calls → IP address checking.

“We’ve had instances where the manager’s like, that’s not who I interviewed. Like, I don’t think that’s the same person.”
